5 Signs Your Garage Floor Needs Resurfacing

Don't wait until your garage floor becomes a safety hazard. Recognizing early warning signs can save you money and prevent more extensive damage. Here's what to look for and when to take action.

1. Cracks and Chips

What to Look For:

  • Fine hairline cracks
  • Wide structural cracks
  • Chipped or broken edges
  • Spalling (surface flaking)

Why It Matters:

Cracks allow moisture penetration, leading to further damage. They can also be trip hazards and make the garage look neglected.

Action:

  • Hairline cracks: Can be filled and coated
  • Large cracks: May need epoxy injection before coating
  • Severe damage: Full resurfacing recommended

2. Oil and Chemical Stains

Common Culprits:

  • Motor oil leaks
  • Gasoline and antifreeze
  • Paint and solvents
  • Battery acid

The Problem:

Untreated concrete is porous and absorbs chemicals. Old stains are nearly impossible to remove from bare concrete.

Solution:

Epoxy coating creates an impermeable barrier. Future spills wipe up easily, and the coating hides existing stains.

3. Dusting and Powdering

What It Looks Like:

Fine gray or white powder accumulating on the floor, especially when you sweep. The concrete surface feels chalky or loose.

Why It Happens:

  • Concrete degradation
  • Poor initial cure
  • Chemical exposure
  • Moisture problems

The Fix:

Epoxy coating penetrates and seals the surface, stopping the dusting and protecting the concrete beneath.

4. Surface Unevenness

Signs:

  • Puddles forming after cleaning
  • Uneven wear patterns
  • Settled or sunken areas
  • Raised sections

Causes:

  • Poor initial pour
  • Soil settlement
  • Water damage
  • Heavy equipment

Resolution:

Self-leveling epoxy can correct minor unevenness. Severe cases may need concrete grinding first.

5. Peeling Existing Coating

What You See:

  • Paint or old coating bubbling
  • Flaking and peeling
  • Discoloration
  • Worn traffic patterns

Root Causes:

  • Poor surface preparation
  • Low-quality coating
  • Moisture issues
  • Age and wear

The Solution:

Remove old coating completely and apply professional-grade epoxy with proper prep. This ensures long-lasting results.

The Resurfacing Process

Step 1: Assessment

Professional inspection to identify all issues and determine best approach.

Step 2: Preparation

  • Crack repair
  • Grinding or shot blasting
  • Cleaning and degreasing
  • Moisture testing

Step 3: Application

  • Primer coat
  • Base epoxy layer
  • Color flakes (optional)
  • Clear topcoat

Step 4: Curing

  • 24 hours for light traffic
  • 72 hours for vehicles
  • 7 days for full cure

Benefits of Resurfacing

  • Protection: Seals and protects concrete
  • Appearance: Like-new look
  • Safety: Eliminates trip hazards
  • Value: Increases property value
  • Maintenance: Much easier to clean
  • Durability: Lasts 10-20 years

Don't Wait Too Long

Addressing floor issues early is more cost-effective than waiting. Minor problems become major repairs if ignored.
